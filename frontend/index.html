About

Brand intelligence that tells you what to do next.

Most monitoring tools show you what people are saying. Signalwatch tells you what it means and what to do about it.

It pulls live mentions from 13 sources, runs pattern analysis across all of them, and produces a single briefing with one recommended action. No dashboard to interpret. No report to commission.

Built for brand managers, consultants, and agencies who need to move quickly.

How it works
01
Collect
14 sources queried simultaneously. News, reviews, forums, video, academic papers, and regulatory filings. Last 90 days only.
02
Filter
Every result is scored for relevance. Anything with no keyword match is removed before analysis begins.
03
Analyse
Each result is checked for co-occurring commercial signals. Price and switching mentioned together in the same post carries more weight than either word alone.
04
Decide
The strongest patterns go through an algorithm + AI layer. Together, they produce a two-sentence briefing and one specific action for the next 48 hours. The signal comes from real market activity.
05
Go deeper
Four specialist agents continue working after your results arrive. They investigate angles the initial scan missed, track competitor moves, check regulatory filings, and surface academic context. Findings stream in as they complete.

Help Centre

How to use Signalwatch

Written by the team, not generated. If something here doesn't answer your question, email support@signalwatch.in and a person will reply.

See examples
A SaaS company tracking competitor complaints
They want to know every time someone complains about a rival's pricing or support, across Reddit, news and review sites. They search:
(Salesforce OR HubSpot) AND (pricing OR "too expensive" OR "customer service")
A PR team monitoring a brand crisis
Something has gone wrong and they need to track every mention as it happens, without getting buried in unrelated noise. They search:
"Brand Name" AND (scandal OR recall OR lawsuit OR apology) NOT careers
An agency finding warm leads for a client
They need people actively asking for recommendations in a specific category, not just browsing. They search:
("looking for" OR "recommend" OR "alternatives to") AND "project management"
Boolean search

AND

Both terms

OR

Either term

NOT

Just one term

AND — both words must appear
A result is only included if it contains every word joined by AND.
Salesforce AND pricing
OR — either word can appear
A result is included if it contains at least one of the words. Use brackets when combining with AND.
(Salesforce OR HubSpot) AND pricing
NOT — excludes a word
Any result containing the word after NOT is removed entirely. Useful for filtering out noise like job postings.
Salesforce NOT careers
"..." — exact phrase
Words inside quotes must appear together in that exact order, not scattered separately in the result.
"customer service" AND Salesforce
